The pitch for AI in education writes itself: a patient, personalised tutor for
every learner, adapting in real time to how a child is doing. The technology to
do a version of this already exists. So why does so much of it feel hollow?

Because personalisation is a product problem, not a model problem — and the
hardest part isn't adapting the content. It's deciding **who the system serves**
and **where the human stays in the loop**.

## The trap: optimising the wrong metric

An adaptive engine that maximises "questions answered correctly" will quietly
steer a child toward whatever keeps the score climbing. That's engagement, not
learning. It can manufacture the *feeling* of progress while hollowing out the
struggle that actually builds understanding.

When I sketched **GuruBot**, an adaptive assistant that tunes story difficulty
and pacing to a child's performance, the design tension was immediate: make it
*too* smooth and you remove the productive friction learning depends on.

## Three design commitments

1. **Adapt the pace, protect the struggle.** Personalisation should remove
   *irrelevant* difficulty (confusing UI, mismatched reading level), not the
   *desirable* difficulty that produces learning.
2. **Keep an adult in the loop.** A parent dashboard or teacher view isn't a
   nice-to-have — it's the mechanism that keeps an opaque model accountable to
   someone who knows the child.
3. **Be honest about what the model doesn't know.** A child's bad day, a noisy
   home, a concept that needs a human metaphor — the system should defer, not
   bluff.

## Personalisation as scaffolding, not substitution

The framing that helps me most: AI in education should behave like
**scaffolding** — temporary support that helps a learner reach something they
couldn't yet reach alone, and then gets out of the way. Scaffolding that never
comes down isn't support; it's dependence.

> The goal isn't a machine that teaches the child. It's a machine that makes the
> teacher and the parent more present, not less.

That's the product I want to build in this space — and it's why the
[MyGuru](/work/myguru) and [AI Companion](/work/ai-companion) explorations on
this site lead with the parent and the story, not the algorithm.
