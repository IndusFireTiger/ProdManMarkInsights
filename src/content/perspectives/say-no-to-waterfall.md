When I wanted teams to move from waterfall habits to genuine iterative delivery,
I didn't write a process memo. I ran a campaign — "Say NO! to Waterfall" — and
treated the change itself as a product:

- **A value proposition**, in the team's terms: less rework, earlier feedback,
  fewer death-march launches.
- **Early adopters** who could prove it on a real initiative, not a pilot
  sandbox.
- **Proof points** that travelled — visible wins other teams wanted to copy.

More to come: the specific rituals that stuck, the ones that didn't, and how to
fold customer input into iteration without diluting the product vision.
