> **Focus:** EdTech · Mobile UX · MVP thinking. An independent exploration of how
> to teach cultural learning to digitally-native children in a format that
> actually engages them.

## Context

Children today are digitally native, but they have little exposure to Indian
mythology and values in formats that hold their attention. MyGuru is a concept
app that teaches cultural learning through short interactive stories,
flashcards and AI-guided quizzes.

## Problem

Engaging, age-appropriate cultural content is scarce — and static content loses
children quickly. The opportunity: pair storytelling with light gamification so
learning feels like play and *sticks*.

## Research

- **Competitive scan:** Byju's, Epic, Duolingo ABC.
- **Personas:** parents (25–45) and children (5–10) — two users, one product.
- **Key insight:** interactive content materially boosts both engagement and
  recall versus passive formats.

## Solution overview

- Short, animated **story modules**.
- **Voice-based quizzes** for pre-readers.
- **Flashcards and streak badges** to build a daily habit.
- A **parent dashboard** so the adult stays involved.

## MVP features

| Feature | Why it's in the MVP |
| --- | --- |
| Story cards | Core engagement loop |
| Quiz engine | Validates that learning happened |
| Streak tracker | Reinforces the daily habit |
| Parent dashboard | Keeps the buying adult involved |

## Metrics

DAU · retention · module completion rate · parent satisfaction.

## Learnings

- **Start small, iterate fast** — the MVP exists to test the engagement loop,
  nothing more.
- **Storytelling builds emotional connection** that raw drills never will.
- **Gamification sustains engagement** — but only in service of the learning,
  not as a substitute for it.
