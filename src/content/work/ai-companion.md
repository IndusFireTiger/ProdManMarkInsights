> **Focus:** AI Product Management · Ethics · Personalisation. An exploration of
> how to define an AI feature responsibly — combining personalisation with
> guardrails that keep a human in the loop.

## Context

Children learn at different paces, and static quizzes disengage them quickly. The
concept: **GuruBot**, an AI companion that adapts quiz difficulty and storytelling
pace dynamically to each child's performance.

## The problem worth solving

Adaptation is easy to build and easy to get wrong. Tune purely for "correct
answers" and the system optimises for the feeling of progress rather than real
learning. The product question is *what to adapt, and what to protect.*

## Feature concept: GuruBot

- Adjusts **difficulty** as the child demonstrates mastery.
- Adjusts **storytelling pace** to maintain engagement without rushing.
- Surfaces a **signal to the parent** when the child is stuck, rather than
  silently smoothing it over.

## Responsible-AI design principles

1. **Adapt the pace, protect the struggle** — remove irrelevant difficulty, keep
   the productive kind.
2. **Keep an adult in the loop** — the parent dashboard is the accountability
   mechanism for an opaque model.
3. **Defer when uncertain** — the system should flag what it doesn't know, not
   bluff past it.

## What it demonstrates

- **AI use-case definition** — scoping a model to a specific, bounded job.
- **Flow design** — how adaptation, content and the parent signal interconnect.
- **Responsible product thinking** — designing the guardrails *before* the
  capability.
